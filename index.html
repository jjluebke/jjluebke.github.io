ollier masonry

Project type:
Private mobile app
Plaform:
iOS
Stack:
Javascript/HTML on Cordova, NodeJS and Postgres on Heroku
Contributions:
End-to-end design, development and distribution
Challenge:
Masonry company needs to be able to manage workforce of more than 60 employees at many job sites. Previously, this work was handled by a single individual on paper.

Summary:

The app and service allow for several supervisors to log in to create jobs, assign workers to those jobs, and track resources including time and automated job site reports. Non-privileged employees can view the app, to see when they are scheduled to work, at which job, and who with. The app is distributed in a closed system via the Apple Enterprise program.

Office administrators can log-in to a web-based reporting page, where they can review timesheets for a given job/employee as well as review daily job reports, which serve as a historical record of work preformed and any issues encountered.

pigeon

Project type:
Public project on GitHub
Plaform:
iOS
Stack:
Swift, NodeJS and SocketIO
Contributions:
End-to-end design, development
Challenge:
Develop an UAV controller using a mobile phone with ground to vehicle communication over wireless networks in place of traditional radio signal.

Summary:

Pigeon is a personal, public project focusing on making it easier to control and fly UAVs. Pigeon is an app that runs on a phone, replacing the need for propritary hardware and external sensors. Setup and control is all preformed through remote software and all communication is over TCP/IP.

Pigeon is not yet airborne, however is very close. The video above depics a recent "bench" test, demonstrating compensation based on craft orientation. Unfortunately, evidence from these tests indicate that bluetooth communication from the phone to the motors is simply not fast enough to prevent oscillations in compensation.
